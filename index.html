Research

I research language and multimodal communication in interaction from various perspectives, with Systemic Functional Linguistics as a predominant framework. I also investigate the intersection of language and technology (i.e., communication in immersive environments, human-computer interaction and computer-mediated communication) and other applied contexts (e.g., Higher Education pedagogy, design, and neurology.).

I am happy to discuss opportunities for research collaboration and postgraduate student supervision. Feel free to get in touch using the 'Contact' section of this webpage.

If you cannot access copies of my research with the links provided, please e-mail me and I will endeavour to assist!

Teaching

I teach and lead the undergradaute English Language and Linguistics programme at UWE Bristol. This includes curriculum development, assessment design and management, and co-designing the programme with students to ensure a robust and adequate syllabus for an increasingly interdisciplinary world.

I use a range of pedagogic techniques that I have honed over 13 years as an educator to provide a varied, engaging and supportive learning environment. I am a strong advocate of social and interactive teaching methods that employ a range of academic and applied skills, ensuring that formative and summative assessments are used appropriately while promoting a focus on 'the learning' rather than on 'the exam.' This is supplemented with appropriate pastoral support at all academic levels.

I am a Fellow of the Higher Education Academy as FHEA, and I am working towards becoming a Senior Fellow. I work with the UWE Bristol's Academic Practice Directorate on the Postgraduate Certificate of Academic and Professional Practice, mentoring new lecturers and guiding them in their applications towards Associate Fellow or Fellow of the Higher Education Academy.
